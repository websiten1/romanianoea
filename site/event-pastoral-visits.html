News · Archpastoral Visits

Archpastoral Visits · 2025

His Eminence Archbishop Nathaniel continues to visit parishes across the United States and Canada — offering blessings, receiving confessions of faith and meeting with clergy and parish councils.

Throughout the year

From coast to coast

In the course of the pastoral year, His Eminence has served hierarchical Divine Liturgies, blessed new altars and iconostases, consecrated newly ordained clergy, and met with parish councils in parishes large and small.

Selected visits · 2025

  • Winter — visits to parishes in the Detroit and Great Lakes region
  • Great Lent & Holy Week — services at the Vatra chapel and regional parishes
  • Paschal season — visits and hierarchical liturgies across the Midwest
  • Summer — feast of Ss. Constantine & Helen pilgrimage at the Vatra; camps; AROY gatherings
  • Autumn — visits to Canadian parishes and the 93rd Episcopate Congress in Chicago
  • Nativity season — parish visits and the issuance of the annual Nativity Pastoral Letter

Purpose of the visits

The Archbishop's visits are not administrative tours — they are acts of archpastoral care: the hierarch serving the Liturgy with the priest and people, praying with and for the parish, meeting the faithful at coffee hour and encouraging the catechumens, the young and the sick.

Parishes interested in scheduling an archpastoral visit are invited to contact the Chancery at (517) 522-4800.
