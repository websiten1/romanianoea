Bishop Andrei (Hoarște)

Auxiliary (Vicar) Bishop of the Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America and Canada — consecrated at the Vatra Românească on 1 February 2025.

His Grace

Servant of God, Helper of His Eminence

His Grace, Bishop Andrei, serves as Auxiliary Bishop of the Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America and Canada, assisting His Eminence Archbishop Nathaniel in the pastoral care of the parishes, monastic communities and diocesan ministries.

Formed in the Romanian monastic tradition and grounded in pastoral service, His Grace brings to the Episcopate a life of prayer, liturgical attention and quiet care for clergy and faithful alike — especially the young.

Monastic and priestly life

Tonsured a monk in the Romanian Orthodox tradition and later raised to the rank of archimandrite, Father Andrei served in parish ministry and as a spiritual father to many before his election to the episcopacy. His labors included catechesis, hearing confessions, pastoral visits, and the quiet, steady work of monastic obedience.

Election as Auxiliary Bishop

Following the prayerful discernment of the Episcopate's Clergy & Laity Congress and the Holy Synod of the Orthodox Church in America, the Very Reverend Archimandrite Andrei (Hoarște) was elected to serve as Auxiliary (Vicar) Bishop of the Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America.

Archpastoral Consecration · 1 February 2025

On Saturday, 1 February 2025, at the Vatra Românească in Grass Lake, Michigan, the Archimandrite Andrei was consecrated bishop in the presence of a multitude of clergy, monastics and faithful gathered from across the Episcopate.

The Divine Liturgy was served by hierarchs of the Orthodox Church in America, joined by His Eminence Archbishop Nathaniel, with the participation of sister Orthodox jurisdictions. The Vatra chapel overflowed with prayer, Romanian and English chant, and the joy of a diocese welcoming a new shepherd.

Immediately following his consecration, His Grace was greeted by clergy and faithful at a reception held at the Vatra, where he received the greetings of the Episcopate and was presented with the pastoral staff of his office.

Present ministry

As Auxiliary Bishop, His Grace assists the Archbishop in archpastoral visits, in the consecration of altars, the ordination of clergy and the tonsuring of monastics, and in representing the Episcopate at sister jurisdictions and inter-Orthodox gatherings. He is particularly attentive to the youth ministries of the diocese — the Vatra camps, AROY and the parish youth programs — and to the life of the Episcopate's monastic communities.
