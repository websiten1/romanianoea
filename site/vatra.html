The Vatra Românească

The Romanian Hearth — spiritual and administrative center of the Episcopate in Grass Lake, Michigan, since 1937.

A Romanian Hearth in America

The heart of the Episcopate

The Vatra Românească — the “Romanian Hearth” — is the historic property of the Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America, situated on rolling farmland in Grass Lake, Michigan, less than an hour from Detroit and Ann Arbor.

Donated to the Episcopate in 1937, the Vatra has, for nearly a century, been the seat of the Archbishop, the home of the Chancery Office, the gathering place for youth and pilgrims, and the resting place of generations of Romanian-American Orthodox faithful.

Buildings & Grounds

Stone, wood and prayer

The Vatra encompasses several historic buildings and natural landmarks:

  • Bishop's Residence — the historic main house, seat of the Archbishop
  • Chancery Office — administrative center of the diocese
  • Historic chapel — daily Divine services for the Vatra community
  • St. Mary Cemetery — resting place of clergy, monastics and faithful
  • Camp grounds — cabins, dining hall, lake, woods and chapel
  • Conference and meeting facilities
Through the Years

A short timeline of the Vatra

1929

Founding of the Episcopate

The Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America is canonically organized as a diocese, gathering the Romanian-American parishes founded in the early decades of the 20th century.

1937

The Vatra is established

The Grass Lake property is donated to the Episcopate. From the beginning it is conceived as a “vatră” — a “hearth” — a Romanian Orthodox spiritual home in the New World.

1950s – 70s

Growth of camps and pilgrimages

Camp Vatra and the patronal pilgrimage of Sts. Constantine and Helen become annual gathering points for thousands of faithful from across North America.

1980s – 2000s

Diocesan ministries flourish

AROY, ARFORA, ARCOLA and the diocesan publication Solia · The Herald shape the lay life of the Episcopate. The Vatra hosts annual Episcopate Congresses and clergy retreats.

2010s

Heritage assessment

After decades of intense use, a comprehensive assessment is commissioned for the Bishop's Residence and supporting buildings — confirming the urgent need for major restoration work.

2020s

Vatra Generations Project launched

A multi-year capital campaign begins: front porch reconstruction, chimney tuck-pointing, roofing, exterior siding, modern wiring, insulation and ongoing infrastructure work.

2025

A new bishop at the Vatra

His Grace Bishop Andrei (Hoarște) is ordained Auxiliary Bishop of the Episcopate at the Vatra on February 1, 2025.

2026

Pascha at the Hearth

Archbishop Nathaniel issues the Paschal Pastoral Letter — “Christ is Risen! A Light to the Nations” — distributed to every parish of the Episcopate.

Capital Project

The Vatra Generations Project

“For our generation. For the next. For the One whom we serve.”

The Vatra Generations Project is the Episcopate's long-term effort to preserve the historic Bishop's Residence and the supporting heritage buildings. Phase work includes front porch restoration, chimney tuck-pointing, roof replacement, new exterior siding, modern wiring, insulation and infrastructure upgrades.

Every donation contributes directly to keeping the Vatra alive as a place of worship, formation and gathering for the next generation of Romanian Orthodox faithful in North America.

Visiting the Vatra Românească

Pilgrims are warmly welcomed at the Vatra throughout the year, especially for the patronal feast of Sts. Constantine and Helen (May 21), the diocesan pilgrimage, the Episcopate Congress, and the summer camp sessions.
