Youth, Camps & AROY

Forming the next generation of Romanian Orthodox Christians through faith, friendship and service — at the Vatra and across America and Canada.

A Living Tradition

Faith for the next generation

Each summer hundreds of children, teens and young adults gather at the Vatra Românească for prayer, catechesis, recreation and community life rooted in the Romanian Orthodox tradition. Throughout the year, AROY chapters and parish youth groups continue this work in every corner of the Episcopate.

From the first Sign of the Cross at Camp Vatra Juniors to leadership in AROY, from monastic pilgrimages to college fellowship — the youth of the Episcopate are received as “the Church not of tomorrow but of today.”

Summer at the Vatra

Our Summer Camps

Four distinct camp programs, each with its own age range, patron saint and rhythm of life — held at the Vatra Românească in Grass Lake, Michigan.

Ages 8 – 12

Camp Vatra · Juniors

The Vatra’s flagship camp for children — an unforgettable first encounter with the rhythm of monastic, communal Orthodox life in a Romanian setting.

  • Daily Divine services and sung prayers
  • Catechesis and lives of the saints
  • Sports, swimming, hiking and crafts
  • Romanian songs, dance and culture
Ages 13 – 17

Camp Vatra · Seniors

A deeper formation week for teens — discussion, leadership, friendships that often endure for life. Many AROY leaders begin here.

  • Catechesis on faith, vocation and discernment
  • Liturgical singing and reading workshops
  • Outdoor and team activities
  • Evening discussions with clergy and elders
Young men · 18+

Camp St. Anthony the Great

A week of brotherhood, prayer and serious engagement with Orthodox Christian manhood. Patroned by St. Anthony the Great, father of monasticism.

  • Akathist services and ascetical readings
  • Talks on vocation, marriage, monastic life
  • Manual labor and service at the Vatra
  • Time in nature and silence
Young women · 18+

Camp St. Mary

A week dedicated to the formation of young Orthodox women, under the patronage of the Most Holy Theotokos. Prayer, sisterhood and reflection.

  • Daily prayer and akathist hymns
  • Talks on vocation, marriage, monastic life
  • Service and works of mercy at the Vatra
  • Crafts, music and shared meals
AROY

American Romanian Orthodox Youth

AROY is the official youth organization of the Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America. Local chapters in our parishes form a national network of Orthodox young people committed to faith, fellowship and service across the United States and Canada.

AROY hosts the annual Labor Day Weekend Conference & Sports Tournament — a beloved tradition combining liturgy, conferences, basketball, soccer and friendships that endure across generations.

What we cultivate

The pillars of our youth ministry

I

Worship

Daily prayer, liturgy and the cycle of feasts at the heart of camp life.

II

Catechesis

Sound teaching of the faith from the Scriptures and the Holy Fathers.

III

Heritage

The richness of the Romanian Orthodox tradition — language, song, hospitality.

IV

Fellowship

Lifelong friendships forged across parishes, states and provinces.

V

Service

Works of mercy, manual labor and stewardship of the Vatra grounds.

VI

Vocation

Discerning marriage, monastic life and service to the Church and the world.

Year-round ministry

Beyond the summer

Parish youth groups

Every ROEA parish is encouraged to host an active youth group — meeting weekly or monthly for prayer, study, service and fellowship. Local clergy and parents work together to provide age-appropriate formation in the faith.

Pilgrimages and retreats

Throughout the year, AROY chapters and parish youth groups undertake pilgrimages to the Vatra, to the monasteries of the Episcopate, and to other holy places across North America.

College and young-adult ministry

Through the Department of Youth and Young Adult Ministries, the Episcopate supports college-age and young-adult ministries in cooperation with Orthodox campus fellowships and sister jurisdictions.

Sunday School & religious education

The Department of Religious Education provides catechetical curricula and resources for Sunday Schools across our parishes — for children, teens and adult inquirers alike.
