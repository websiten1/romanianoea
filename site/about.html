About the Episcopate

History

A Romanian Orthodox witness in North America

The Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America (ROEA) is a diocese serving Romanian-American Orthodox Christian faithful in the United States and Canada. Its administrative center — the Vatra Românească ("Romanian Hearth") — is located in Grass Lake, Michigan, on a property donated to the Episcopate in the early years of its life as a diocese.

ROEA traces its roots to the early twentieth century, when Romanian immigrants established parishes from the East Coast through the Midwest and into the Canadian prairies. Organized as a diocese in 1929, it has continued to grow through ordained clergy, monastic foundations, summer camps, and lay auxiliary organizations.

Canonical Status

ROEA is a self-governing Romanian-tradition diocese in canonical communion with — and an integral diocese of — the Orthodox Church in America (OCA). It cooperates fraternally with the Romanian Patriarchate and with sister Orthodox jurisdictions through the Assembly of Canonical Orthodox Bishops.

The Vatra Românească

The Vatra is the heart of the Episcopate: the seat of the Archbishop, location of the diocesan chancery, the Bishop's Residence, the historic chapel and St. Mary Cemetery, and the campgrounds for diocesan youth and young adult ministries.

Through the Vatra Generations Project, faithful from across North America support major capital restoration of the historic buildings — including the Bishop's Residence, the chimneys, roofing, porches, and modern infrastructure upgrades.

Summer Camps and Youth Ministry

  • Camp Vatra for Juniors
  • Camp Vatra for Seniors
  • Camp St. Anthony the Great
  • Camp St. Mary

Each summer hundreds of children, teens and young adults gather at the Vatra for prayer, catechesis, recreation and community life rooted in the Romanian Orthodox tradition. Throughout the year, AROY (American Romanian Orthodox Youth) chapters in our parishes continue this work in every corner of the Episcopate.
