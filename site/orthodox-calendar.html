    Appendix

    Church Regulations — 2026

    The principal disciplines of the liturgical year, as observed throughout the Romanian Orthodox Episcopate of America.

    Lenten periods & fast days

    • Wednesdays and Fridays throughout the year (except times when designated as Fast Free)
    • Eve of Theophany (Monday, January 5)
    • Beheading of St. John the Baptizer (Saturday, August 29)
    • Lifting of the Holy Cross (Monday, September 14)
    • Paschal Fast — Great Lent: Monday, February 23 – Saturday, April 11
    • Holy Apostles Peter & Paul Lent: Monday, June 8 – Sunday, June 28
    • Dormition of the Mother of God Lent: Saturday, August 1 – Friday, August 14
    • Nativity of the Lord Lent: Sunday, November 15 – Thursday, December 24

    Non-liturgical days & different liturgies

    Non-liturgical days (no Holy Liturgy is celebrated): Wednesday and Friday during Cheesefare week (February 18 & 20); Monday and Tuesday of the first week of Great Lent (February 23 & 24); Holy and Great Friday (April 10).

    Liturgy of St. Basil the Great is celebrated 10 times each year: January 1 and 5; March 1, 8, 15, 22 and 29; April 9, 11; December 24.

    Liturgy of the Pre-Sanctified Gifts is celebrated on weekdays during Great Lent (Monday – Friday), beginning with the Wednesday of the first week of Lent until Great and Holy Wednesday inclusive, with the exception of the feast of the Annunciation (March 25).

    Liturgy of St. John Chrysostom is celebrated on all other days of the year.

    When memorial services are prohibited

    On all Sundays throughout the year; on Imperial Feasts (on Ascension of the Lord a special memorial service is celebrated for fallen soldiers); during the days of the week (Monday-Friday) of Great Lent; during non-liturgical days as indicated in the calendar; in the period from Palm Sunday until St. Thomas Sunday (April 5-19); in the period from the Nativity of the Lord to Theophany (December 25 – January 6).

    When marriages are prohibited — 2026

    During all lenten periods and fasting days; from the Monday after Meatfare Sunday for the Fast of Holy Pascha until the Sunday of St. Thomas inclusive (February 16 – April 19); during the Fast of the Holy Apostles Peter and Paul (June 8 – 28); during the Dormition Fast (August 1 – 14); on the Feast of the Beheading of St. John the Baptist (August 29); during the Nativity Fast (November 15 – December 24); on the eve and on the days of the Great Feasts of the Lord (February 1–2; May 20–21; May 30 – June 1; August 15; September 13–14); from Christmas to Theophany (December 25 – January 6).

    When marriages are prohibited — 2027

    During all lenten periods and fasting days: on all Wednesdays and Fridays; from the Monday after Meatfare Sunday for the Fast of Holy Pascha until the Sunday of St. Thomas inclusive (March 8 – May 9); during the Fast of the Holy Apostles Peter and Paul (June 22 – 28); during the Dormition Fast (August 1 – 14); on the Feast of the Beheading of St. John the Baptist (August 29); during the Nativity Fast (November 15 – December 24); on the eve and on the days of the Great Feasts of the Lord (February 1–2; June 9–10; June 19–21; August 15; September 13–14); during the period from Christmas to Theophany (December 25 – January 6).
