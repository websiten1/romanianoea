Restoration at the Episcopal Residence

A major phase of the Vatra Generations capital project: preserving the historic Episcopal Residence for the next century of ministry.

2025 · On-site

What is being done

The Episcopal Residence, donated to the Episcopate in 1937 and home to the Archbishops of the diocese ever since, has been subject to almost a century of Michigan winters, summer storms, and the honest wear of a life of prayer and hospitality. The current phase of work is addressing the deepest structural and envelope needs first.

Current scope of work

  • Exterior — full replacement of the historic siding with period-appropriate materials
  • Roof & chimneys — re-roofing and chimney repair
  • Porches — reconstruction of the front and side porches using traditional profiles
  • Electrical — a complete rewire of the building to current code
  • Insulation — blown-in insulation throughout exterior walls and attic
  • Drainage & grading around the residence perimeter

Approach

The work is proceeding under the oversight of the Vatra Generations building committee, in close coordination with the Chancery and with tradespeople familiar with historic Michigan homes. Wherever possible, original wood and detail are being preserved; where replacement is necessary, period-appropriate profiles are specified so that the house continues to look and feel like what it has always been.

Progress & next steps

Early phases of exterior repair and envelope work are under way. Subsequent phases will address the interior systems, finishes and the Chancery wing. A detailed project report is published annually to Congress delegates and is available through the Chancery.

How to help

The Vatra Generations Project is funded entirely by the generosity of parishes and faithful across the Episcopate. Every gift — large or small — moves the work forward.
